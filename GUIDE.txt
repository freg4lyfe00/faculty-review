================================================================================
  FACULTY ANNUAL REVIEW TOOL — GUIDE
  Plain-language overview of what this is, where it lives, how to use it,
  and why it works the way it does.
================================================================================


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT IS THIS?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This is a single web page that a faculty member fills out once a year to
document their work — publications, presentations, grants, mentoring activity,
and self-reflection on goals. It replaces a Word doc or PDF form with something
that saves itself, tracks changes, and produces clean outputs for the division
chief.

It lives as a single .html file. You can open it in any web browser by
double-clicking it, just like a Word document. No internet connection is
required to use it. No account, no login, no software to install.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHERE DOES IT LIVE?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

There are two ways to access it:

  1. Online (shared link):
     https://faculty-review-alpha.vercel.app
     Anyone with this link can open it in a browser. Good for demos or
     getting started. Note: changes made here are NOT saved to the cloud —
     the file saves to your own computer (see "Saving" below).

  2. On your computer (your personal copy):
     The file is named something like:
       faculty-review-Jane-Smith-2024-25.html
     Once you save it (using the "Editable File" button), it lives on your
     computer and contains all your data inside it. This is your working copy.
     Keep it somewhere you can find it — your Desktop or Documents folder.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HOW TO GET STARTED
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FIRST TIME:
  1. Open the file in a web browser (double-click it, or visit the link above).
  2. A "Welcome" box will appear asking for your name, career stage, and
     division. Choose your career stage from the dropdown (Assistant Professor,
     Associate Professor, or Professor) and click "Begin →".
  3. Your name now appears in the top bar. You are ready to go.

RETURNING:
  Simply open your saved .html file. Everything you entered before will
  be right there. If the browser detects a more recent backup, it will
  offer to restore it.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE TABS — WHAT EACH ONE IS FOR
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The row of tabs across the top takes you to each section of the review.

  OVERVIEW
  ─────────
  A read-only summary of your entire year on one page. It shows:
    • Your name, career stage, and division at the top
    • Four count boxes: total publications, presentations, active grants,
      and mentees (with a note if the count went up or down from last year)
    • Your goals from last year, carried forward from Self-evaluation
    • Short tables of your publications, presentations, grants, and mentees
      (up to 5 entries each, with a clickable link to see all of them)

  Nothing to fill in here — it updates automatically as you add data in
  the other tabs. Think of it as your "at a glance" dashboard.

  TRENDS
  ───────
  Charts that compare your activity across multiple academic years — how
  your publication count, presentations, active grants, and mentees have
  changed over time. This tab only shows meaningful data once you have
  more than one year saved in the file.

  PUBLICATIONS
  ─────────────
  Add each paper, review, editorial, or book chapter you worked on this
  year. For each one you enter: the full citation, whether it is published /
  submitted / in revision / in preparation, and what type of work it is.
  Click "+ Add" to start a row, fill in the fields, and click Save.

  PRESENTATIONS
  ──────────────
  Add talks, posters, and workshops you gave this year. For each one:
  the date (like "Mar 2026"), the event name, the type (invited talk /
  oral abstract / poster / workshop), and the title.

  GRANTS
  ───────
  Add any grants you submitted, received, or are working on. For each one:
  the date, funding source (NIH, AHRQ, etc.), grant type (R01, K23, etc.),
  the full title, and the status (awarded / under review / not funded).

  MENTORS & MENTEES
  ──────────────────
  Two parts:
    • My mentor(s) — the people who mentor YOU. Add their name and role.
      These fields are always live — just type and they save automatically.
    • My mentees — the people YOU mentor. Add each person's name, their
      level (undergraduate, medical student, graduate student, resident,
      fellow, or faculty), and a note about their project.

  SELF-EVALUATION
  ────────────────
  Your written reflection. This is the heart of the annual review. It has
  several sections:

    Contributions & highlights — What are you most proud of this year?
    AY [prior year] goals — review — Goals you set last year, now with
      space to note your progress, any barriers, and feedback received.
    AY [this year] goals — Goals you are setting for the coming year,
      and what support you need.
    Leadership roles — Any leadership positions you hold and your plans.
    Academic promotion — Whether you are working toward promotion and
      where you are in that process.
    CARE+ & salary review — A checkbox confirming you reviewed your
      salary sheet, plus any outstanding questions.
    Professional development — How the department can support your career.
    Division Chief comments — This section is for the chief to fill in
      after you submit. It appears blank for you.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SAVING YOUR WORK
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

This tool does NOT save automatically to a server. Your data lives inside
the file itself. Here is how saving works:

  AUTOMATIC BACKUP (every 60 seconds)
  The tool quietly saves a backup copy to your browser's local storage —
  a temporary holding area on your computer. If you close the tab by
  accident, the next time you open the file the browser will offer to
  restore from that backup.

  MANUAL SAVE — "Editable File" button (bottom left of the screen)
  This is your main save action. Clicking it downloads a fresh copy of
  the .html file with all your current data baked in. Replace your old
  copy with this new one. Treat it like hitting "Save" in Word.

  The "Unsaved changes" badge (amber, bottom left) pulses whenever you
  have made changes that have not yet been saved to a file. It disappears
  after you click "Editable File."

  TIP: Save after every working session. The file is small and fast to
  download. Keep only the most recent copy.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE ACADEMIC YEAR SWITCHER
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

In the top right corner you will see a pill-shaped button showing the
current academic year (e.g. "2024-25"). If you have multiple years saved
in the file, you can click between them to view or edit past years.

The "+" button creates a new academic year. When you do this, the goals
you set for the coming year in your Self-Evaluation are automatically
carried over into the new year's "goals review" section — so you never
have to retype them.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SHARING WITH THE DIVISION CHIEF
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

There are two ways to share your completed review:

  "READ-ONLY" button (bottom left)
  ──────────────────────────────────
  Downloads a full interactive dashboard — the same interface you use,
  but with all editing removed. The division chief can click through every
  tab, switch between years, and see the Trends charts, but cannot change
  anything.
  File is named: dashboard-readonly-[your name]-[date].html
  Share this the same way you would share a Word document (email, folder).

  "SUBMIT" button (bottom right)
  ───────────────────────────────
  For the official submission. Before it works, it checks that you have:
    • Written something in Contributions & highlights
    • Added at least one publication
    • Noted progress on at least one goal
  If anything is missing, it tells you what to complete first.

  Once submitted, two files are automatically downloaded:
    1. Your editable file (updated with a "Submitted" badge)
    2. A clean, print-ready summary of this year's review
       (named: annual-review-[your name]-[year]-export.html)

  The "Submitted" badge also appears next to the year pill in the header
  so it is easy to see which years have been officially submitted.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ADDING / EDITING / DELETING ENTRIES
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Every data tab (Publications, Presentations, Grants, Mentors & Mentees)
works the same way:

  ADD:    Click the "+ Add" button in the top right of the section.
          A new row opens in edit mode with blank fields.
          Fill them in and click "Save", or press Enter.
          Press Escape to cancel without saving.

  EDIT:   Hover over a row — a pencil icon (✎) appears on the right.
          Click it to open that row for editing.
          Click "Save" or press Enter when done.

  DELETE: Hover over a row — an × icon appears on the right.
          Click it. The row turns amber and asks "Delete this entry? Yes / No"
          Click Yes to confirm, or No to keep it. No pop-up windows.

  LONG LISTS: Tables show 8 entries at a time by default. If you have more,
          a "Show all" link appears at the bottom of the table. Click it to
          see every entry, or "Show fewer" to collapse it again.

For the Self-Evaluation tab, all fields are always open for typing —
just click into any box and start writing. Changes save automatically
to the file's memory as you type (you still need to click "Editable File"
to write them to your saved copy).


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
IMPORTING DATA FROM A JSON FILE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

If you have data prepared in a spreadsheet or database, you can import it
into the tool all at once instead of entering each row by hand.

  HOW TO IMPORT:
  1. Click the "Import data" button in the top-right corner of the header
     (it sits next to the year pills).
  2. A file picker opens. Select your .json file.
  3. The tool reads the file and loads the data into the matching year.
     A confirmation message appears at the top of the screen.

  WHAT GETS IMPORTED:
  The import supports four sections: publications, presentations, grants,
  and mentees. Any combination of these is fine — you do not need all four.
  Mentor and self-evaluation data cannot be imported this way; those must
  be filled in by hand.

  If the year in the file already exists in the tool, you will be asked to
  confirm before anything is replaced. If it is a new year, it is created
  automatically.

  WHAT THE JSON FILE LOOKS LIKE:
  The file is a plain text file ending in .json. Think of it like a very
  structured list. Here is a small example showing all four sections:

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ {                                                                   │
  │   "academicYear": "2025-2026",                                      │
  │                                                                     │
  │   "publications": [                                                 │
  │     {                                                               │
  │       "citation": "Smith J. My paper title. J Example. 2026;1:1.", │
  │       "status": "published",                                        │
  │       "type": "original-article"                                    │
  │     }                                                               │
  │   ],                                                                │
  │                                                                     │
  │   "presentations": [                                                │
  │     {                                                               │
  │       "date": "Mar 2026",                                           │
  │       "event": "National Example Conference",                       │
  │       "type": "invited-talk",                                       │
  │       "title": "My talk title"                                      │
  │     }                                                               │
  │   ],                                                                │
  │                                                                     │
  │   "grants": [                                                       │
  │     {                                                               │
  │       "date": "Sep 2025",                                           │
  │       "source": "NIH",                                              │
  │       "grantType": "R01",                                           │
  │       "title": "Full grant title here",                             │
  │       "status": "awarded"                                           │
  │     }                                                               │
  │   ],                                                                │
  │                                                                     │
  │   "mentees": [                                                      │
  │     {                                                               │
  │       "name": "Pat Lee",                                            │
  │       "level": "graduate-student",                                  │
  │       "comments": "Working on example project"                      │
  │     }                                                               │
  │   ]                                                                 │
  │ }                                                                   │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  REQUIRED FIELD: "academicYear" must always be present (e.g. "2025-2026").
  Without it, the file will be rejected.

  VALID VALUES FOR DROPDOWN FIELDS:
  These fields only accept the exact values listed below — no other text.

    Publications — status:
      "published"  "submitted"  "in-revision"  "in-preparation"

    Publications — type:
      "original-article"  "editorial"  "review"  "guidelines"  "book-chapter"

    Presentations — type:
      "invited-talk"  "oral-abstract"  "poster"  "workshop"

    Grants — status:
      "awarded"  "under-review"  "not-funded"

    Mentees — level:
      "undergraduate"  "medical-student"  "graduate-student"
      "resident"  "fellow"  "faculty"

  TIP: A sample file called "sample json.json" is included alongside this
  guide. You can open it in any text editor to see the format, then copy
  and edit it to match your own data.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PRINTING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Use your browser's built-in Print command (Ctrl+P on Windows, Cmd+P on Mac).
The tool has a print layout built in — buttons, navigation, and editing
controls are hidden automatically, and only the content of the current tab
is printed with clean formatting.

For a complete printed record of all sections, use the Read-Only download
and print from that file, since it contains all tabs visible at once.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHY IT WORKS THIS WAY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

The tool was intentionally built as a single file — no database, no server,
no account required — for a few reasons:

  • Privacy: Your data never leaves your computer unless you choose to
    share the file. Nothing is stored online.

  • Durability: The file works the same way in 10 years as it does today.
    There is no service that can shut down and take your records with it.

  • Portability: You can email it, put it on a USB drive, or keep it in
    a shared folder. Opening it requires only a web browser, which everyone
    already has.

  • Self-contained history: Every year's data lives in the same file.
    When you start a new academic year, last year's data is still there.
    The Trends tab gets richer the longer you use it.
